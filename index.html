üìã SAT Overview & Test Structure

The SAT is a standardized test widely used for college admissions. The current format includes:

Test Sections:

Reading (65 minutes): 52 questions testing reading comprehension across literature, history, social studies, and science passages.

Writing & Language (35 minutes): 44 questions focusing on grammar, usage, and rhetoric in context.

Math (80 minutes total): 58 questions split between calculator and no-calculator sections, covering algebra, geometry, and data analysis.

üí° Pro Tip

Familiarize yourself with the test format before test day. Knowing what to expect reduces anxiety and helps you manage time effectively.

Grammar Rules to Master:

Subject-Verb Agreement: Ensure subjects and verbs match in number, even with complex sentence structures.

Pronoun Usage: Check that pronouns clearly refer to specific nouns and match in number/gender.

Parallel Structure: Items in lists or comparisons should follow the same grammatical pattern.

Modifier Placement: Place descriptive words and phrases next to what they modify.

Rhetoric and Style:

‚Ä¢ Choose words that match the passage's tone and style

‚Ä¢ Look for the most concise, clear expression

‚Ä¢ Consider the logical flow and organization of ideas

‚Ä¢ Evaluate whether additions improve or detract from the passage

‚ö° Quick Check

When in doubt, read the sentence aloud in your head. Often, the correct answer will "sound right" because it follows natural speech patterns.
